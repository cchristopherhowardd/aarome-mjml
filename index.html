April 21, 2020
Dear Friend,
For 125 years the American Academy in Rome has created a special environment for scholars and artists, providing them with space and a community in which to create new work and explore ideas. In the face of this global health crisis, we reaffirm our mission to support artists and scholars, living and working together in Rome.
As many of you know, the Academy has closed and members of the community have returned safely home. Beginning in February, the Academy took a number of steps to safeguard the health and safety of residents and staff: modifying library and meal services, canceling lectures and public events, and finally, suspending all activity. Under the leadership of director John Ochsendorf, AAR staff took extraordinary care assisting Fellows with travel, housing, and other logistical issues in the face of this unprecedented event. We appreciate their efforts and the resilience of the entire community.
While the current need in the world is great, the Academy is also grappling with new challenges. We need your continued support and I ask you to please consider a gift today. Whether you have spent time at the Academy as a Fellow, Resident, Visiting Artist/Scholar, or friend — watched the livestream of Conversation/Conversazioni, attended an exhibition or performance, or used the library or archives for research — you understand this institution’s unique value.
The Academy helps ensure the continuity of intellectual and creative output from the many Fellows who have lived and worked in Rome. In these difficult times, we are reminded of the importance of community and the greater impact of ideas.
We wish you and your families safe haven and good health in the days ahead.
Sincerely,
Mark Robbins, FAAR’97
President and CEO
